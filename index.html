OUR MISSION

Stimulate sustained interest and enthusiasm in computer science; train students the necessary computing knowledge and skills required to provide effective and timeless computer service; train students with a solid background in computer science and knowledge that are required for postgraduate studies and research; conduct research to advance the state of the art in computer science and integrate its innovations into other disciplines.

OUR VISION

To become the preeminent department in research and teaching, driving economic and developmental growth within the nation through the effective utilization of information and communication technology (ICT).

BRIEF HISTORY OF OUR DEPARTMENT

In 2015, upon its inception, the University established two colleges: the College of Management and Social Sciences (COMASS) and the College of Natural and Applied Sciences (CNAS). Within CNAS, four departments were initiated. The Computer Science program originated as a component of the Department of Physical Sciences. Over the years, the Department of Computer Science has matured into a distinctive academic entity renowned for producing highly skilled graduates in the field. Furthermore, the Department offers a comprehensive program leading to the Bachelor of Computer Science degree. While the department has experienced some growth in student enrollment, expansion remains a primary objective. Lectures are conducted in designated lecture halls on campus, ensuring an optimal learning environment. The program's structure aligns with the standards set forth by the National Universities Commission (NUC) for Minimum Academic Standards and adheres to global requirements for a Computer Science degree. Faculty members within the Department are actively engaged in cutting-edge computer research, positioning the department at the forefront of technological advancements. This dedication ensures the continued production of top-tier graduates poised to excel in various sectors, including banking, industry, academia, and private enterprise.

ABOUT US

The Department of Computer Science at Chrisland University has a rich history dating back to the inception of the university in 2015. Initially established as an arm of the Department of Physical Sciences within the College of Natural and Applied Sciences (CNAS), the Computer Science program has since evolved into a distinguished academic division dedicated to producing highly skilled professionals in the field. Over the years, the department has witnessed steady growth in student enrollment, reflecting its reputation for delivering quality education and training. Our program is designed to offer students a comprehensive understanding of both the theoretical principles and practical applications of computer science. Through hands-on training and engaging coursework, students develop the skills necessary to tackle diverse challenges and innovate in various domains. As a testament to our commitment to academic excellence, our program adheres to the National Universities Commission (NUC)'s Benchmark for Minimum Academic Standards and meets global requirements for a degree in Computer Science. At the heart of our department's philosophy is the belief in fostering a passion for computer science and equipping students with the knowledge and skills needed to excel in today's digital age. Our objectives are clear: to instill in students an appreciation for the capabilities of computer science, provide an intellectually stimulating learning experience, and cultivate a broad and balanced education in the field. We aim to develop in our students a range of transferable skills applicable to various industries and contexts, ensuring they are well-prepared for future endeavors. Our vision is ambitious yet achievable: to become the leading department in research and teaching, driving economic growth through information and communication technology (ICT). To fulfill this vision, we are dedicated to stimulating sustained interest in computer science, providing top-notch training to our students, and conducting groundbreaking research that pushes the boundaries of the field. By integrating innovation into our curriculum and collaborating across disciplines, we aim to make significant contributions to both academia and industry. In essence, the Department of Computer Science at Chrisland University is not just a place of learning; it is a hub of innovation, discovery, and transformation. We invite you to join us on this exciting journey as we shape the future of computer science and make a positive impact on society.

THE COURSE SYSTEM

Chrisland University, Abeokuta operates a Course system. This is a system in which subjects are broken down into one or more convenient sections called Course, which are taught and examined within a semester. A Course is assigned a specific number of lectures and practical/tutorial hours and the total number assigned to it will determine its unit value. A Course unit is defined as fifteen hours of lecture (one lecture per week) or forty-five hours of practical work (3 hours of practical work per week) for 15 weeks. Regulations guiding the operation of the Course system in the College of Natural and Applied Sciences are specified in the prospectus.

The University also operates the Semester system under which full academic session is divided into two semesters. A full semester consists of 15 weeks of lectures plus practical, after which examinations are taken.

COURSE CREDIT SYSTEM

This should be understood to mean a ‘quantitative system of organization of the curriculum in which subject areas are broken down into unit courses which are examinable and for which students earn credit(s) if passed’. The courses are arranged in progressive order of difficulty or in levels of academic progress, e.g. Level or Year I courses are 100,101 etc., and Level II or Year II courses are 200, 202 etc. The second aspect of the system is that courses are assigned weights called Credit Units.

REGULATIONS GOVERNING THE OPERATIONS OF THE COURSE SYSTEM

i. Chrisland University, Abeokuta shall adopt the Course System in conducting all its academic programmes.

ii. A course is any part or whole of a subject which can be completed through lectures, tutorials and/or practical with a specified number of hours within a semester.

iii. Courses are normally weighed in terms of Course Units ranging from one to six.

iv. A Course is defined as subject matter completed in fifteen lecture hours or forty-five practical hours. Whenever tutorials are included in the instruction, they are rated as lecture.

REQUIREMENT ENTRY INTO COMPUTER SCIENCE

Direct Entry Requirements:

NCE in Computer Science with any of Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, Integrated Science, Economics or Two ‘A’ Level Passes in Physics and Mathematics or Chemistry.

UTME Requirements:

Five ‘O’ Level SSCE/WASC Credit Passes in English Language, Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry plus any other science subjects.

UTME Subjects:

The entry requirements shall be at least credit level passes in five subjects including English Language, Mathematics, and Physics to form the core subjects with credit in any other two relevant science subjects, at the Senior Secondary School Certificate or its equivalent. In addition, an acceptable pass in the University. Candidates with two A-level passes (graded A-E) at the Advanced Level in one or more relevant subjects (Chemistry, Mathematics, and Physics) may undertake the three – year degree programme into 200-level.

Waivers and Special Consideration/Other Qualifications:

Holders of OND/NCE (minimum upper credit) in relevant subjects may be considered for Direct Entry to 200 Level provided they satisfy the JME requirements
